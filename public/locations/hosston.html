Commercial Roofing in Hosston, LA

Highway 71's Small Buildings Need a Roofer Who Shows Up

Hosston sits on Highway 71 in the northern reach of Caddo Parish, close enough to the gas fields that a fair amount of the commercial traffic through town is oilfield service trucks, and far enough from Shreveport that owners here often can't get a contractor to return a call for a small job. We keep Hosston on our regular route rather than treating it as an afterthought.

What's Along the Highway Here

The commercial buildings that face Highway 71 through Hosston are mostly small — a country store, a farm and feed supply, maybe a small office building used by a gas-field service company, a school building set back from the road. None of it is large-scale by Shreveport standards, but every one of those roofs is protecting something somebody depends on, whether that's inventory, records, or a classroom.

Roofing material out here skews metal, same as the rest of north Caddo Parish, with the school building being the most likely candidate for a low-slope membrane section over classrooms or a gym addition. We treat each type differently — a metal store roof gets checked for fastener and seam issues, a membrane section on a school gets checked for seam integrity and drainage the way we would anywhere else.

Gas-Field Traffic Changes What We Watch For

A service office or yard building tied to gas-field work sees more heavy truck traffic nearby than a typical small-town storefront does, which means more vibration and more incidental damage risk from vehicles working close to the building. We check eave trim, gutters, and lower wall panels for impact damage on these buildings specifically, in addition to the usual roof-top items, because the ground-level traffic around a service building can do as much damage as weather does.

We've also noticed that these buildings tend to change hands or change operators more often than a family-owned store on the same stretch of highway, since service contracts and field activity shift over a few years. A new operator moving into an existing building often has no roofing history on the structure at all, so we start those relationships with a full baseline inspection rather than assuming whatever the last tenant knew got passed along.

These buildings also tend to run HVAC harder than a simple store would, given office equipment and sometimes server or communications gear tied to field operations, so roof-mounted equipment curbs and penetrations get closer attention than they would on a plain retail building.

A Hosston Inspection Checklist

  • Fastener and seam condition on metal panel roofs
  • Impact damage to eave trim and gutters from ground-level truck traffic
  • Roof curb and penetration condition around HVAC and communications equipment
  • Membrane seam integrity on any low-slope sections, like a school gym addition
  • Drainage function during heavy rain, especially at additions tied into older rooflines

School Buildings Get a Different Level of Attention

A school roof carries a different kind of consequence when it fails — a leak over a classroom means displaced students and a maintenance call that can't wait for a normal repair queue, and a roof section that's out of service during a storm is a safety issue as much as an inconvenience. We treat inspection requests on school buildings with that urgency, and we're direct about what can be patched short-term versus what needs a scheduled replacement before the next school year.

Weather on This Part of Highway 71

North Caddo Parish gets hit by the same severe spring weather line as the rest of the Ark-La-Tex, and open highway frontage with little windbreak means wind and hail hit these roofs with less to slow them down first. We check storm damage calls out here with the same urgency we would anywhere else in our service area — a small building's owner shouldn't wait longer for a callback just because the building is smaller or farther from Shreveport.

Summer heat is the other factor that gets overlooked on these smaller buildings. A metal roof with no radiant barrier or insulation package runs considerably hotter inside than a treated roof does, which matters for a store owner paying to cool a small retail space or a school trying to keep classroom temperatures reasonable through a north Louisiana summer. When we're quoting a recover on an older building, we bring up the coating and insulation options that actually change that number, instead of only the ones that patch the leak.

Hosston Roofing Questions From Local Owners

Do you actually come out this far, or is it mostly a Shreveport-area operation?

We cover Hosston as part of our regular north Caddo Parish route. It's not out of the way for us, and we don't treat it as a lesser priority than closer jobs.

Our gas-field office building has some dented gutters from truck traffic. Is that a roofing issue?

It can become one if the gutter is pulling away from the fascia or trapping water against the roof edge. Worth having it checked even if there's no leak yet.

How fast can you respond if a school roof starts leaking during the school year?

We treat school leak calls as priority calls given the disruption involved. Get in touch and we'll get out as quickly as the situation requires.

Can you match materials on an older metal storefront roof?

Usually, yes, or close enough to blend visually. We'll check panel profile and gauge before quoting.

What's your service area besides Hosston?

We cover the whole north Caddo Parish corridor along Highway 71, plus the broader Shreveport-Bossier region.
